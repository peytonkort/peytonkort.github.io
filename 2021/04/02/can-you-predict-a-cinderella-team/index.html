Can you predict a Cinderella team?

2021-04-02

Teams like VCU and UCLA have had incredible runs in March. Back in 2011, VCU made it all the way to the Final Four as an 11 seed. UCLA this year has made that exact same run as an 11 seed as well.

But what do these two teams have in common. Both were a team that had to play in the First Four, which are the lowest at-large teams facing off against each other. There are a total of four of the lowest at-large teams each year. The First Four started in 2011, in which two 11 seeded match ups, and two 16 seeded match ups, making it a total of four games. Only two of these First Four teams have made it past the Sweet 16. They are the 2011 VCU Rams and the 2021 UCLA Bruins.

Let’s compare these two teams stats throughout the seasons of there historic runs.

Looking at UCLA and VCU stats throughout each of there seasons, they had a lot of similarities. But they were no where near the top of the nation in any given stat. Two stats they were very similar in was average margin of victory (4.8 and 5.2) and assist to turnover ratio (1.29 and 1.28).
