Does a Quarterback's Hand Size Matter?

2021-04-20

One of the best measurements in the NFL Draft Combine is the hand size measurements. Hand size measurement at the NFL Combine is done by measuring the tip of the thumb to the tip of the pinky finger with the hand outstretched.

As a fan this is one of the most laughable measurements in the draft. Who is going to pick there future star quarterback based off there hand size? So this got me thinking… does hand size really matter? Let’s look at the stats and see.

First I want to narrow my data set down to quarterbacks that have had a lot of playing time over this season. So I want to filter out with QBs over 1000 passing yards. Also I want to see the mean of hand sizes of all the quarterbacks that played in the NFL last season. I can use this to make comparison’s down the road.

qbstats <- qbstats %>%
  mutate(FmbG = Fmb/G) %>%
  mutate(IntG = Int/G)
qbstatsmorefiltered <- qbstats %>%
  filter(`Pass Yds` > 1000) %>%
  arrange(desc(`Pass Y/G`))

The statistics I want to compare hand size too is fumbles per game, interceptions per game and passing yards per game. I’m using per game data because some of these players didn’t play every game during the season. This should balance out the comparisons for those who didn’t play a full season.

Hand size should play a part in fumbles, because of the ability to grip the football if you have bigger hands. Therefore you should fumble less than players with smaller hands. Also I’m going to compare interceptions, because they are a part of turnovers just like the fumbles. Lastly I chose passing yards per game because I want to see if hand size has any affect on that stat. Passing is also the most important stat of a quarterback in the NFL, obviously. So I have to see if there is a correlation.

Looking at this chart it is very evenly mixed of different hand sizes and the fumble per game average.

Let’s compare some of the quarterback’s with small hands and big hands. All these quarterbacks started 16 games except for Goff who started 15.

In conclusion, does a quarterbacks hand size affect there overall game? From my results I would say no. Despite Joe Burrow leading the NFL in fumbles per game while having the smallest hands at 9.0 in my data set, other guys that have an above average hand size also fumble the ball quite a bit and aren’t too far off Burrow. You look at Josh Allen who fumbles so much more than Ryan Tannehill and Jared Goff, despite having over an inch bigger of a hand. I believe that the hand size measurements is just another measurement to get us talking about the NFL Draft and the future quarterbacks of the league. It does not affect the players stats.
