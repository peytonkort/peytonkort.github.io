Does a Quarterback's Hand Size Matter?

2021-04-20

One of the best measurements in the NFL Draft Combine is the hand size measurements. Hand size measurement at the NFL Combine is done by measuring the tip of the thumb to the tip of the pinky finger with the hand outstretched.

As a fan this is one of the most laughable measurements in the draft. Who is going to pick there future star Quarterback based off there hand size? So this got me thinking… does hand size really matter? Let’s look at the stats and see.

First let’s see the mean of hand sizes of all the Quarterbacks that played in the NFL last season. I can use this to make comparison’s down the road. Also I want to see QBs that have played a lot of time this year. So I am going to filter out for just Quarterbacks with 2 or more starts on the 2021 season.

qbstats <- qbstats %>%
  mutate(FmbG = Fmb/G) %>%
  mutate(IntG = Int/G)
mean(qbhandsize[["Hand Size"]])
## [1] 9.554688
mean(qbstats[["Fmb"]])
## [1] 3.2
mean(qbstats[["IntG"]])
## [1] 0.5746301
mean(qbstats[["FmbG"]])
## [1] 0.3026362
qbstatsfiltered <- qbstats %>%
  filter(GS > 1)

A stat that get’s compared to QB hand sizes are fumbles. The smaller the hands are the more fumbles a particular Quarterback is going to have. Let’s put this theory to the test and compare hand size and fumbles on a scatterplot.

ggplot(qbstatsfiltered, aes(x=`Hand Size`, y=FmbG)) + 
  geom_point() + 
  geom_hline(yintercept= 0.3026362, color="blue") + 
  geom_vline(xintercept=9.554688, color = "red")

Let’s narrow this down even more and show the QB’s with at least 1000 passing yards on the year.
